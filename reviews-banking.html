How to Switch Banks: 5-Step Guide

1

Open Your New Account

Choose your new bank and open accounts online (10-15 minutes). Fund with a small initial deposit.

2

Update Automatic Transactions

Switch direct deposits, bill payments, and subscription services to your new account (allow 30 days).

3

Transfer Your Money

Move most funds to new account, leaving enough in old account to cover any pending transactions.

4

Monitor Both Accounts

Watch both accounts for 1-2 months to ensure all transactions have successfully transferred.

5

Close Old Account

Once all transactions are cleared (60-90 days), formally close your old account in writing.

üìã Pro Tip: Use Account Switching Services

Many banks (Ally, Capital One, Chase) offer free switching services that automatically transfer your recurring payments and direct deposits. This can save hours of manual work.

Banking Questions Answered

Are online banks safe?

Yes, equally as safe as traditional banks. Reputable online banks are FDIC-insured (up to $250,000 per depositor), use bank-level encryption (256-bit SSL), and have multiple security layers including 2-factor authentication.

How do I deposit cash with an online bank?

Options include: 1) Use a linked traditional bank account, 2) Purchase money orders (Walmart, post office) and mobile deposit, 3) Use retail partners (GreenDot at Walmart, CVS, etc.), 4) Keep a local bank account for cash deposits.

What happens if my online bank fails?

Your money is protected by FDIC insurance. The FDIC typically arranges for another bank to take over accounts by the next business day. You'll have access to insured funds within days.

Should I use multiple banks?

Yes, a "banking ecosystem" approach is smart. Use online banks for high-yield savings, traditional banks for cash/check deposits, and specialized banks for specific needs (international, business, etc.).
