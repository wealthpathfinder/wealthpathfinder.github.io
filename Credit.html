Credit & Debt FAQs

How long does it take to build good credit from scratch?

You can establish a credit score in about 6 months, but building good credit (670+) typically takes 12-18 months of consistent, responsible credit use. Excellent credit (740+) usually takes 2+ years of perfect payment history and low utilization.

Will checking my credit score lower it?

No, checking your own credit score is a "soft inquiry" and doesn't affect your score. Only "hard inquiries" from lenders when you apply for credit can temporarily lower your score by a few points.

How much will paying off a credit card improve my score?

Paying down credit card balances can improve your score quickly, often within 1-2 billing cycles. The impact depends on how much you're reducing your credit utilization. Going from 50% to 9% utilization could boost your score by 40+ points.

Should I close old credit cards I don't use?

Generally no, unless they have high annual fees. Closing old accounts reduces your total available credit (hurting utilization) and shortens your credit history (hurting credit age). Keep them open with occasional small purchases.
