Which Method Should You Choose?

â„ Choose Snowball If:

  • You need motivation to stay on track
  • You have many small debts
  • Quick wins keep you motivated
  • Interest rates are similar across debts

ðŸ” Choose Avalanche If:

  • You want to save the most money
  • You have high-interest credit card debt
  • You're disciplined and don't need quick wins
  • Interest rates vary significantly

Debt Payoff FAQs

What's the difference in savings between methods?

The avalanche method typically saves 10-30% more in interest compared to snowball. However, snowball users often stick with their plan longer due to psychological wins.

Should I pay off debt or invest?

Generally, pay off high-interest debt first (>7-8%). For lower interest debt, you might get better returns investing, but being debt-free provides financial security.

What if I can't make extra payments?

Start with minimum payments and focus on increasing income or reducing expenses. Even $25-50 extra per month can significantly reduce your payoff time.

Should I consider debt consolidation?

Consolidation can simplify payments and potentially lower interest rates, but it doesn't reduce the total amount owed. Use our debt consolidation calculator to compare.
