Frequently Asked Questions

Is cash back taxable?

Generally no. The IRS considers cash back rewards as a rebate or discount on purchases, not taxable income. However, there are exceptions:

  • Sign-up bonuses: Typically not taxable if tied to spending requirement
  • Bank account bonuses: Often taxable (Form 1099-INT)
  • Business cards: Cash back reduces deductible business expenses
  • 1099-MISC: You might receive one for very large bonuses

When in doubt: Consult a tax professional, especially for bonuses over $600.

Do cash back cards hurt my credit score?

Short-term impact: Yes, when you apply (hard inquiry, new account).

Long-term impact: Can help your score if used responsibly.

Factors That Help Your Score
  • Payment history (35%): On-time payments help
  • Credit utilization (30%): More credit = lower utilization
  • Credit age (15%): Older accounts help average age
  • Credit mix (10%): Different types of credit help
Factors That Hurt Short-Term
  • Hard inquiries (10%): -5 points each, lasts 2 years
  • New accounts: Lowers average age temporarily
  • High utilization: Over 30% hurts score

Net effect: For most people with good credit habits, cash back cards improve scores over time.
