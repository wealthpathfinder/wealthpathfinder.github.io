Frequently Asked Questions

Is my money safe with online brokers?

Yes, protected up to $500,000. Reputable brokers are SIPC-insured (Securities Investor Protection Corporation), which protects up to $500,000 in securities and $250,000 in cash per account type. Additional insurance from Lloyd's of London or similar often provides extra protection up to $150 million.

Can I have accounts at multiple brokers?

Absolutely, and many investors should. Different brokers excel in different areas. Common strategy: Use Fidelity/Schwab for long-term/retirement accounts, Interactive Brokers for active trading/international, and a robo-advisor for automated investing. Just track overall asset allocation across accounts.

How much do I need to start investing?

As little as $1 with fractional shares. Platforms like Fidelity, Charles Schwab, and Robinhood offer fractional shares, allowing you to buy portions of expensive stocks. Many robo-advisors have $0-$100 minimums. The key is starting small and investing consistently.

What happens if my broker goes out of business?

Your securities are protected and transferred. SIPC steps in to transfer customer accounts to another solvent brokerage within days. You won't lose your investments‚Äîthe process is designed to be seamless. This has happened several times (Lehman Brothers, MF Global) with customers made whole.
