Per-Card Utilization vs Total Utilization: Both Matter

Many people don't realize that credit scoring models look at TWO types of utilization:

ðŸ’³ Per-Card Utilization

How much of each individual card's limit you're using. Maxing out one card hurts your score even if your total utilization is low.

Example:

Card A: $900/$1,000 limit = 90% utilization âŒ
Card B: $100/$10,000 limit = 1% utilization âœ…

Result: Score damage from Card A's high utilization

ðŸ“Š Total Utilization

The combined utilization across all your cards. This is the number most people focus on, but it's only half the picture.

Example:

Card A: $2,000/$5,000 = 40%
Card B: $3,000/$5,000 = 60%
Total: $5,000/$10,000 = 50%

Result: Both individual and total utilization are too high

Aim for 1-9% on each individual card AND 1-9% total utilization. If you can't achieve both, prioritize keeping individual cards below 30% as that often has a bigger impact.

Why it's bad: Reduces your total available credit, which increases your utilization ratio. Older accounts also help your credit age.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I improve my score by lowering utilization?

Very quickly! Utilization has no memory in most scoring models. If you lower your utilization from 80% to 9%, you could see a 50-100 point improvement in just 30-45 days once the new balances are reported to credit bureaus.

Does utilization include installment loans (mortgage, car loans)?

No, utilization only applies to revolving credit (credit cards, lines of credit). Installment loans have their own metrics (payment history, remaining balance vs original amount) but don't affect utilization ratio.

Should I pay off my credit cards multiple times per month?

Yes, if you want to optimize utilization. Making multiple payments ensures lower balances are reported at statement time. However, if you pay in full monthly anyway, one payment is fine for your finances - multiple payments are just for score optimization.

What's more important: paying down balances or increasing limits?

Paying down balances is usually better because it saves you interest and doesn't require credit checks. However, if you can get limit increases without hard pulls, that provides immediate utilization improvement without spending money.
