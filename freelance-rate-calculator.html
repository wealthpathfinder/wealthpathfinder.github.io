Frequently Asked Questions

Should I charge hourly or project-based rates?

It depends on the project type:

Hourly Pricing

Best for: Ongoing work, uncertain scope, clients who change requirements

Example: $85/hour for content strategy consulting

Project Pricing

Best for: Well-defined projects, fixed deliverables, experienced freelancers

Example: $3,500 for complete website redesign

Value-Based Pricing

Best for: High-impact work, ROI-driven projects, expert consultants

Example: 15% of revenue generated from campaign

How do I raise rates for existing clients?

Follow this 4-step process:

  1. Give 30-60 days notice: "Starting January 1, my rates will increase to..."
  2. Explain the value: "This increase reflects my additional expertise in [new skill] and the improved results I've delivered..."
  3. Offer options: "I can continue at current rate for 3 more months, or we can lock in a 6-month retainer at 10% discount off new rate."
  4. Be prepared to lose some: 10-20% of clients may leave. Replace them with higher-paying ones.

What's the difference between freelance and agency rates?

Typical rate multipliers:

Freelancer $50-150/hour Individual expert
Small Agency (2-5 people) $100-250/hour Team with project manager
Medium Agency (6-20 people) $150-400/hour Multiple specialists, processes
Large Agency (20+ people) $250-600+/hour Full-service, brand reputation

Your Rate Action Plan

Step 1: Calculate Your True Rate

Use the calculator above to determine your minimum, target, and ideal rates based on your financial goals.

Step 2: Research Your Market

Compare with industry benchmarks and adjust for your specific niche, skills, and location.

Step 3: Test Your Rate

Quote your new rate to 3 prospective clients. Track conversion rates and feedback.

Step 4: Implement Gradually

New clients: new rate immediately. Existing clients: raise rates with proper notice and justification.

Step 5: Review Quarterly

Re-evaluate your rates every 3-6 months based on demand, skills growth, and market changes.

ðŸ’¡ Remember: Price Anchoring Works

Clients often equate price with quality. Charging 20% below market can signal inexperience. Charging 20% above (with justification) can position you as premium. The sweet spot is usually 5-15% above average for your experience level.
