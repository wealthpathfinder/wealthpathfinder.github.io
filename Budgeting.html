Budgeting FAQs

How much should I actually be saving each month?

Most experts recommend saving 15-20% of your gross income for retirement, plus additional amounts for other goals. The 50/30/20 rule suggests 20% total for savings and debt repayment. Start with what you can and gradually increase.

What's the difference between a budget and a spending plan?

A budget is often seen as restrictive ("what you can't spend"), while a spending plan is proactive ("what you choose to spend"). The mindset shift can make financial planning feel more empowering and less limiting.

How do I budget for irregular expenses?

List all irregular expenses (car maintenance, gifts, subscriptions) and their annual costs. Divide by 12 and save that amount monthly in separate sinking funds. This prevents budget surprises.

What should I do if I consistently overspend in certain categories?

First, determine if your budget is unrealistic or if it's a spending habit issue. If the budget is realistic, try the envelope system for that category, use cash-only, or implement a 24-hour waiting period for non-essential purchases.
