Frequently Asked Questions

How many credit cards should I have?

2-4 cards is optimal for most people. This gives you category bonuses while keeping management simple. More than 5-6 becomes difficult to track and may hurt your credit score from too many inquiries/new accounts.

Do credit card applications hurt my credit score?

Temporarily, yes. Each application causes a hard inquiry (5-10 point drop) and lowers average account age. However, responsible use of new cards typically recovers this within 3-6 months, and long-term benefits (higher total credit limit, payment history) outweigh initial dip.

Should I cancel old credit cards?

Generally no. Old cards help your credit history length and total credit limit (improves utilization ratio). Keep no-fee cards open with occasional small charges. Only cancel cards with high annual fees that don't provide enough value.

What's the best strategy for paying off multiple cards?

Use either the avalanche method (highest interest first) or snowball method (smallest balance first). Combine with a 0% balance transfer card to stop interest accrual while you pay down principal.
