What is a CD Ladder and Why Build One?

A CD (Certificate of Deposit) ladder is a strategy where you purchase multiple CDs with staggered maturity dates. This approach gives you higher returns than traditional savings accounts while maintaining regular access to portions of your money.

Imagine having 5 buckets of money. Each bucket becomes available at different times throughout the year. If you need cash, a bucket is always becoming available soon. If you don't, you refill it for another cycle at current rates.

Advanced CD Ladder Strategies

Barbell Strategy

Invest in very short-term and long-term CDs, skipping middle terms.

Example: 50% in 6-month CDs, 50% in 5-year CDs
Best for: Rate uncertainty - hedge your bets

Bullet Strategy

All CDs mature around the same target date.

Example: Buy 2, 3, 4, and 5-year CDs all to mature in 5 years
Best for: Specific future expense (college, house down payment)

CD + Treasury Ladder

Mix CDs with Treasury securities for tax advantages.

Example: CDs for shorter terms, Treasuries for longer terms
Best for: High tax brackets (Treasuries are state-tax exempt)

CD Ladder FAQs

What happens if I need money before a CD matures?

You can withdraw early but will pay an early withdrawal penalty, typically 3-12 months of interest. This is why you should keep emergency funds separate.

Are CD ladder returns guaranteed?

Yes, the interest rate is fixed for the CD term. Your principal is FDIC insured up to $250,000 per depositor, per bank.

What's better: CD ladder or bond ladder?

CDs are FDIC insured and simpler. Bonds may offer higher returns but have credit risk. Many investors use both for diversification.

How do I handle CD ladder taxes?

You'll receive a 1099-INT form for interest earned each year. Interest is taxed as ordinary income in the year it's credited to your account.
