Investing FAQs

How much money do I need to start investing?

You can start with as little as $100! Many brokerages have no minimums, and fractional shares let you buy pieces of expensive stocks. The key is to start early and invest consistently.

What's the difference between stocks and bonds?

Stocks represent ownership in companies (higher risk, higher potential returns). Bonds are loans to companies or governments (lower risk, fixed returns). Most portfolios should include both.

Should I invest during market downturns?

Yes! Market downturns can be great buying opportunities. Continue investing regularly through dollar-cost averaging - you'll buy more shares when prices are low and fewer when prices are high.

How do I choose between a Roth and Traditional IRA?

Choose Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement (common for young investors). Choose Traditional if you want tax deductions now and expect to be in a lower bracket later.
