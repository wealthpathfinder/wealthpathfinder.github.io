Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need to start investing?

As little as $5. Many apps now offer fractional shares, allowing you to buy portions of expensive stocks. Acorns even lets you start with $0 by investing your spare change from everyday purchases.

Are investment apps safe?

Yes, reputable ones are. Look for apps that are SIPC-insured (protects up to $500,000), use bank-level encryption, and offer two-factor authentication. Always download apps from official app stores.

What should beginners invest in first?

Start with broad market index funds or ETFs like VTI (total US market) or SPY (S&P 500). These provide instant diversification with low fees. Avoid picking individual stocks until you've built a solid foundation.

How do I avoid investment app fees?

Choose commission-free platforms (Robinhood, Webull, Fidelity) or apps with percentage-based fees (Betterment 0.25%) rather than flat monthly fees for small balances. Always read the fee schedule before signing up.
