Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage of my home can I deduct for home office?

Two methods:

Simplified Method

Rate: $5 per square foot (max 300 sq ft)

Max Deduction: $1,500 per year

Best for: Small spaces, minimal records

Actual Expense Method

Rate: Percentage of home used for business

Max Deduction: Unlimited (based on expenses)

Best for: Larger spaces, detailed records

Example: 150 sq ft home office = $750 with simplified method, or potentially more with actual expenses.

Can I deduct meals with clients?

2025 Rules:

  • Client meals (business discussion): 50% deductible
  • Employee meals (office party): 100% deductible
  • Travel meals (overnight business): 50% deductible
  • Entertainment (sports, concerts): 0% deductible

Documentation required: Receipt, date, business purpose, names of attendees, business relationship.

What if I use my personal vehicle for business?

Two methods to choose:

Standard Mileage Rate

2025 Rate: 67 cents per business mile

Includes: Depreciation, maintenance, insurance

Best if: You drive a lot for business

Actual Expense Method

Calculate: Gas, repairs, insurance Ã— business %

Plus: Depreciation deduction

Best if: High vehicle expenses, low mileage

Tip: Track both methods for first year, choose higher deduction.

Your Tax Deduction Action Plan

Monthly Tasks

  • Track all business mileage
  • Save receipts (digital or physical)
  • Categorize expenses in accounting software
  • Review bank/credit card statements

Quarterly Tasks

  • Calculate estimated tax payments
  • Review deduction tracker for missed items
  • Make quarterly tax payments (if required)
  • Back up all tax documents

Annual Tasks

  • Complete Schedule C (Form 1040)
  • File Form 8829 for home office
  • Submit Form 4562 for depreciation
  • Pay any remaining tax balance
  • Plan next year's tax strategy

ðŸ“ The Golden Rule: Document Everything

If you can't prove it, you can't deduct it. Keep:

  • Receipts: Digital scans are acceptable
  • Mileage logs: Date, purpose, start/end odometer
  • Bank/credit card statements: Business expenses highlighted
  • Calendar entries: Business purpose documentation
  • Contracts/invoices: Proof of business relationships

Retention period: Keep records for 3 years from filing date, or 7 years if claiming depreciation.
